# 6. Nonviolent Communication (NVC)

> — Marshall Rosenberg, "Nonviolent Communication"

> [🇰🇷 한국어](../foundations.ko/06_비폭력_대화.md)

---

## The Four-Step Structure

```
Observation → Feeling → Need → Request
```

### 1. Observation
State facts without judgment or evaluation.

- ❌ "You're always late."
- ✅ "You've arrived more than 15 minutes late to the last three meetings."

### 2. Feeling
State the emotion you feel in response to the observation.

- ❌ "I feel like you're being irresponsible." (This is an evaluation, not a feeling.)
- ✅ "I feel anxious and frustrated."

### 3. Need
Name the need behind the feeling.

- ✅ "When meetings start on time, I feel the whole team's time is being respected."

### 4. Request
Make a specific, actionable request — not a demand.

- ❌ "Stop being late." (Demand)
- ✅ "Could you arrive five minutes early starting with the next meeting? If that's difficult, let me know ahead of time."

---

## Core Insight

**Most conflict is a collision of unmet needs.**
When someone is behaving "strangely," ask first: what need might be behind this?

## Practical Points for Managers

- Replacing judgment with observation alone lowers the temperature of a conversation.
- "What was the situation?" is less threatening than "Why did you do that?"
- You need to know your own feelings and needs before you can hear those of others.
